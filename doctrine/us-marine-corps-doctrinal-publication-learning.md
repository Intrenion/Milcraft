## Practice 1: Build competence through deliberate practice

**Problem**  
Knowledge without repeated practice does not produce reliable performance.

**Action**  
Practice important skills until you can apply them under pressure.

**Outcome**  
You perform your duties with greater speed and confidence.

Chapter: The Nature of Learning - Learning and Competence

## Practice 2: Learn faster than the enemy

**Problem**  
Fixed methods fail when conditions and enemy actions change.

**Action**  
Study each experience and quickly adjust your next action.

**Outcome**  
Your unit adapts before the enemy can gain an advantage.

Chapter: The Nature of Learning - The Role of Learning in Warfighting

## Practice 3: Strengthen learning through feedback

**Problem**  
Practice can reinforce mistakes when no one identifies them.

**Action**  
Use timely feedback to correct performance during every learning cycle.

**Outcome**  
Correct skills become stronger with each repetition.

Chapter: The Nature of Learning - Key Principles of Learning

## Practice 4: Work with human limits

**Problem**  
Stress and mental overload reduce attention and memory.

**Action**  
Present difficult material in manageable steps with time for practice.

**Outcome**  
Learners understand and retain more useful knowledge.

Chapter: The Nature of Learning - The Human Dimension and Science of Learning

## Practice 5: Practice solving unfamiliar problems

**Problem**  
War rarely presents problems that match a prepared answer.

**Action**  
Train with changing situations that require independent decisions.

**Outcome**  
Marines can create workable solutions in uncertain conditions.

Chapter: The Nature of Learning - Problem Solving as a Warfighting Capability

## Practice 6: Train the mind for combat

**Problem**  
Physical strength and equipment cannot replace sound judgment.

**Action**  
Develop judgment by studying problems and making decisions under pressure.

**Outcome**  
You use knowledge as an effective combat advantage.

Chapter: The Nature of Learning - The Mind as a Weapon

## Practice 7: Learn every day

**Problem**  
Skills and knowledge weaken when learning stops.

**Action**  
Make study and reflection part of your daily routine.

**Outcome**  
You remain ready for new duties and changing threats.

Chapter: The Nature of Learning - Continuous Learners

## Practice 8: Turn experience into better performance

**Problem**  
Experience has little value when its lessons are ignored.

**Action**  
Apply one clear lesson from each important experience.

**Outcome**  
Daily learning produces steady gains in mission performance.

Chapter: The Nature of Learning - Conclusion

## Practice 9: Make learning a shared norm

**Problem**  
People hide questions and mistakes when the culture discourages learning.

**Action**  
Treat study and honest discussion as normal parts of unit work.

**Outcome**  
The organization learns openly and improves faster.

Chapter: The Culture of Learning - Organizational Culture

## Practice 10: Share responsibility for learning

**Problem**  
Learning fails when people assume that instructors alone own it.

**Action**  
Give every Marine a clear role in personal and unit development.

**Outcome**  
Learning continues throughout the organization.

Chapter: The Culture of Learning - Areas of Responsibility

## Practice 11: Own your professional growth

**Problem**  
Formal training alone cannot prepare you for every future duty.

**Action**  
Study your profession beyond assigned courses and immediate tasks.

**Outcome**  
You become better prepared for greater responsibility.

Chapter: The Culture of Learning - Professional

## Practice 12: Set clear learning expectations

**Problem**  
Learning becomes optional when leaders do not define what they expect.

**Action**  
State the learning behaviors required from every member of the unit.

**Outcome**  
Marines understand that continued development is part of their duty.

Chapter: The Culture of Learning - Expectations

## Practice 13: Reinforce learning through daily behavior

**Problem**  
A learning culture weakens when actions do not match stated values.

**Action**  
Reward curiosity and improvement during routine unit activities.

**Outcome**  
Learning becomes a lasting part of how the unit operates.

Chapter: The Culture of Learning - Conclusion

## Practice 14: Design environments for active learning

**Problem**  
Passive instruction gives learners too little practice in using knowledge.

**Action**  
Create realistic activities that require learners to think and act.

**Outcome**  
Learners can apply knowledge in demanding situations.

Chapter: The Learning Environment - Learning Environments

## Practice 15: Create conditions for honest effort

**Problem**  
Fear of embarrassment prevents learners from asking questions or testing ideas.

**Action**  
Build trust by treating honest mistakes as chances to improve.

**Outcome**  
Learners take useful risks and correct weaknesses sooner.

Chapter: The Learning Environment - Setting the Conditions for Success

## Practice 16: Make learning work anywhere

**Problem**  
Operational demands often limit access to classrooms and formal courses.

**Action**  
Use available time and local resources to continue learning in the field.

**Outcome**  
Development continues during deployments and other demanding operations.

Chapter: The Learning Environment - The Expeditionary Nature of Learning

## Practice 17: Adapt learning to the learner

**Problem**  
One fixed method does not meet every learner's needs.

**Action**  
Adjust instruction based on each learner's progress and experience.

**Outcome**  
Each learner receives the support needed to improve.

Chapter: The Learning Environment - A Learner-Centric Model

## Practice 18: Direct your own learning

**Problem**  
Learners make slow progress when they wait for others to guide every step.

**Action**  
Choose a useful goal and follow a plan to reach it.

**Outcome**  
You can develop needed skills without constant supervision.

Chapter: The Learning Environment - Self-Directed Learning

## Practice 19: Guide learners through practice

**Problem**  
Long explanations do not show whether learners can perform the task.

**Action**  
Coach learners as they practice and solve problems.

**Outcome**  
Learners gain skill through active effort and timely guidance.

Chapter: The Learning Environment - The Role of the Instructor

## Practice 20: Use assessment to improve learning

**Problem**  
Scores alone do not show learners how to improve.

**Action**  
Use assessment results to identify the next skill that needs work.

**Outcome**  
Learners focus their effort on clear performance gaps.

Chapter: The Learning Environment - Assessments as Tools

## Practice 21: Choose technology for a clear purpose

**Problem**  
Technology can distract learners when it does not support the learning goal.

**Action**  
Use technology only when it makes practice or feedback more effective.

**Outcome**  
Digital tools improve learning without replacing sound instruction.

Chapter: The Learning Environment - Technology in Support of Learning

## Practice 22: Connect every part of the learning environment

**Problem**  
Learning suffers when goals and activities do not support each other.

**Action**  
Align the setting with the performance that learners must achieve.

**Outcome**  
The learning environment prepares Marines for real demands.

Chapter: The Learning Environment - Conclusion

## Practice 23: Encourage curiosity and growth

**Problem**  
People stop exploring ideas when leaders punish questions or honest mistakes.

**Action**  
Invite questions and support thoughtful attempts to improve.

**Outcome**  
Marines become more willing to learn and adapt.

Chapter: The Learning Leader - Foster and Encourage

## Practice 24: Teach through leadership

**Problem**  
Orders alone do not develop the judgment needed for future decisions.

**Action**  
Explain the reasoning behind decisions when conditions allow.

**Outcome**  
Marines learn how to make sound decisions on their own.

Chapter: The Learning Leader - Teaching and Leading

**Problem**

**Action**  
Acknowledge uncertainty and seek informed advice.

**Outcome**  
The unit makes decisions with better information.

Chapter: The Learning Leader - Humility

**Problem**  
Leaders lose credibility when their professional skills become weak.

**Action**  
Practice essential technical and tactical tasks throughout your career.

**Outcome**  
You can teach others and lead effectively in difficult conditions.

Chapter: The Learning Leader - Technical and Tactical Proficiency

**Problem**  
Marines will not value learning when leaders neglect their own development.

**Action**  
Show consistent learning through study and reflection.

**Outcome**  
Your example strengthens learning across the unit.

Chapter: The Learning Leader - Conclusion
