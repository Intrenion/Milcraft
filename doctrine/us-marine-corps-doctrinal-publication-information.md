## Practice 1: Treat information as a source of action

**Problem**  
Information has little value when people cannot use it to understand or act.

**Action**  
Collect, interpret, share, and protect information according to a clear purpose.

**Outcome**  
People can make sound decisions from relevant information.

Chapter: Nature of Information - Information Explained

## Practice 2: Treat information as combat power

**Problem**  
Ignoring information can surrender influence and decision space to an opponent.

**Action**  
Consider information effects when planning every military activity.

**Outcome**  
Military actions produce stronger effects on decisions and behavior.

Chapter: Nature of Information - How Important is Information?

## Practice 3: Map the information environment

**Problem**  
Information moves through connected people, systems, and physical conditions.

**Action**  
Identify key audiences, networks, sources, and influences before acting.

**Outcome**  
Plans account for how information will move and affect people.

Chapter: Nature of Information - Characteristics of the Information Environment

## Practice 4: Study how competitors use information

**Problem**  
Competitors use information continuously to gain influence without direct conflict.

**Action**  
Track competitor messages, actions, audiences, methods, and desired effects.

**Outcome**  
The force can recognize and counter hostile influence sooner.

Chapter: Nature of Information - How Competitors Approach Information

## Practice 5: Include information in every operational decision

**Problem**  
Every military action creates information that others can interpret.

**Action**  
Assess the likely meaning of each action before execution.

**Outcome**  
Actions communicate the intended message more consistently.

Chapter: Nature of Information - Conclusion

## Practice 6: Build informational power deliberately

**Problem**  
Information cannot create power unless it changes understanding or behavior.

**Action**  
Combine credible information with actions that support its meaning.

**Outcome**  
Target audiences are more likely to respond as intended.

Chapter: Theory of Information - Informational Power

## Practice 7: Use information to manage uncertainty

**Problem**  
Friction and uncertainty make military decisions slower and less reliable.

**Action**  
Improve friendly understanding while increasing uncertainty for the opponent.

**Outcome**  
Friendly forces act with greater confidence than opposing forces.

Chapter: Theory of Information - Information and the Attributes of War

## Practice 8: Gain an advantage for a specific decision

**Problem**  
More information does not automatically produce a better decision.

**Action**  
Give decision makers the right information before the opponent can respond.

**Outcome**  
Friendly forces can make and execute important decisions first.

Chapter: Theory of Information - What is an Information Advantage?

## Practice 9: Apply information by function

**Problem**  
Information efforts become scattered when their purpose is unclear.

**Action**  
Use information to generate, preserve, deny, and project relevant knowledge.

**Outcome**  
Each information activity supports a defined operational need.

Chapter: Theory of Information - Functions of Information

## Practice 10: Pair cognitive effects with functional effects

**Problem**  
A target may continue to resist even when only its physical capabilities change.

**Action**  
Define the required changes to both the target behavior and the target function.

**Outcome**  
Objectives address what the target can do and chooses to do.

Chapter: Theory of Information - The Cognitive and Functional Components of Military Objectives

## Practice 11: Deceive toward a specific decision

**Problem**  
Deception fails when the false picture does not guide a useful target response.

**Action**  
Present believable information that leads the target toward a chosen wrong decision.

**Outcome**  
The target acts in a way that creates an operational opportunity.

Chapter: Theory of Information - Information and Deception

## Practice 12: Link information effects to objectives

**Problem**  
Information activities waste effort when they are separated from operational goals.

**Action**  
Connect each information effect to a specific objective and target decision.

**Outcome**  
Information contributes directly to mission success.

Chapter: Theory of Information - Conclusion

## Practice 13: Combine information with all forms of action

**Problem**  
Isolated information activities cannot fully shape a contested environment.

**Action**  
Coordinate information capabilities with physical actions and operational timing.

**Outcome**  
Different actions reinforce the same intended effect.

Chapter: Effective Use of Information - Principles of the Information Warfighting Function

## Practice 14: Create information advantages around critical choices

**Problem**  
Broad information superiority is difficult to achieve and maintain.

**Action**  
Focus resources on the information needed for the most important decisions.

**Outcome**  
The force gains useful advantages where they matter most.

Chapter: Effective Use of Information - Achieving Information Advantages

## Practice 15: Adapt information use across competition

**Problem**  
The same information approach will not fit cooperation, competition, and conflict.

**Action**  
Adjust messages, capabilities, and risk to the current level of competition.

**Outcome**  
Information activities remain effective as conditions change.

Chapter: Effective Use of Information - Information Advantages across the Competition Continuum

## Practice 16: Sustain information advantages through assessment

**Problem**  
An information advantage can disappear when audiences or conditions change.

**Action**  
Measure target responses and continuously adjust information activities.

**Outcome**  
Information efforts stay aligned with the changing environment.

Chapter: Effective Use of Information - Conclusion

## Practice 17: Establish information as a distinct warfighting function

**Problem**  
Information effects receive uneven attention when no function is responsible for their integration.

**Action**  
Assign clear responsibility for planning and coordinating information activities.

**Outcome**  
Information receives consistent command attention and resources.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Distinguishing the Information Warfighting Function

## Practice 18: Integrate information with other warfighting functions

**Problem**  
Information plans lose their impact when developed in isolation from other operations.

**Action**  
Include information requirements and effects in every warfighting function.

**Outcome**  
All functions support a shared operational purpose.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Information and the Other Warfighting Functions

## Practice 19: Plan information from the start

**Problem**  
Late information planning cannot shape major operational choices.

**Action**  
Include information on objectives, audiences, effects, and measures throughout planning.

**Outcome**  
Information influences the design and execution of the operation.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Information in the Planning Process

## Practice 20: Focus information resources on priorities

**Problem**  
Limited people, time, and capabilities cannot support every information need equally.

**Action**  
Rank information efforts by their expected contribution to the mission.

**Outcome**  
Critical information needs receive support first.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Prioritizing Information

## Practice 21: Use every available network

**Problem**  
A single force rarely has enough access or credibility to reach every audience.

**Action**  
Coordinate capabilities and information sharing with allies and partners.

**Outcome**  
The combined network gains wider access and greater influence.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Leveraging all Capabilities, and Ally and Partner Networks

## Practice 22: Design exercises for real effects

**Problem**  
Training exercises send observable signals to competitors and partners.

**Action**  
Plan exercise activities to deliberately affect real audiences.

**Outcome**  
Exercises shape outside perceptions while meeting training goals.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Using Training Exercises for Real-World Effect

## Practice 23: Control observable information

**Problem**  
Careless words and actions can reveal intentions or weaken credibility.

**Action**  
Protect sensitive details and follow release rules in every setting.

**Outcome**  
Adversaries gain fewer useful clues from friendly behavior.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Practicing Discipline in the Information Environment

## Practice 24: Align actions with a clear narrative

**Problem**  
Conflicting words and actions weaken trust in the organization.

**Action**  
Use a consistent narrative that matches command decisions and service behavior.

**Outcome**  
Audiences receive a credible and coherent explanation of events.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Command and Service Narrative

## Practice 25: Build information skills across the force

**Problem**  
Information cannot become routine when personnel lack shared knowledge and practice.

**Action**  
Teach and rehearse information concepts through doctrine, training, and education.

**Outcome**  
Personnel apply information principles consistently in operations.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Doctrine, Training, and Education

## Practice 26: Design the force for information demands

**Problem**  
A force built without information needs may lack the required people and capabilities.

**Action**  
Include information on roles, systems, and skills in force development decisions.

**Outcome**  
The future force can operate effectively in contested information environments.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Force Development and Design

## Practice 27: Make information a permanent responsibility

**Problem**  
Information efforts fade when they depend on temporary attention or separate specialists.

**Action**  
Embed responsibility for information in command, planning, training, and force design.

**Outcome**  
The force uses information as a normal part of every mission.

Chapter: Institutionalizing Information - Conclusion
