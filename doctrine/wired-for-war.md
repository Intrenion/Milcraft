## Practice 1: Treat robotic war as a present reality

**Problem**  
Leaders make poor choices when they treat military robots as distant ideas.

**Action**  
Examine how robots already affect combat before setting policy or strategy.

**Outcome**  
Decisions reflect the real capabilities and risks of robotic warfare.

Chapter: The Change We Are Creating - Introduction: Scenes from a Robot War

## Practice 2: Learn from the history of automation

**Problem**  
New machines often revive old questions about control and human purpose.

**Action**  
Study earlier automated weapons and machines before judging current robots.

**Outcome**  
Past successes and failures guide better choices about new systems.

Chapter: The Change We Are Creating - Smart Bombs, Norma Jeane, and Defecating Ducks: A Short History of Robotics

## Practice 3: Understand how robots work

**Problem**  
People cannot assess a robot when they misunderstand its basic parts and limits.

**Action**  
Learn how its sensors, software, controls, power, and physical systems work together.

**Outcome**  
Technical claims become easier to test against actual performance.

Chapter: The Change We Are Creating - Robotics for Dummies

## Practice 4: Plan for rapid technological growth

**Problem**  
Linear forecasts underestimate technologies that improve at an increasing rate.

**Action**  
Review plans often as computing, sensors, and robotics become cheaper and stronger.

**Outcome**  
Plans remain useful as capabilities change faster than expected.

Chapter: The Change We Are Creating - To Infinity and Beyond: The Power of Exponential Trends

## Practice 5: Prepare for the next generation of warbots

**Problem**  
Current systems reveal only a small part of what future military robots may do.

**Action**  
Track emerging designs and test how they could change missions and defenses.

**Outcome**  
Forces prepare before new robotic capabilities reach the battlefield.

Chapter: The Change We Are Creating - Coming Soon to a Battlefield Near You: The Next Wave of Warbots

## Practice 6: Set clear limits on machine autonomy

**Problem**  
Autonomous weapons can make lethal choices faster than humans can review them.

**Action**  
Define which decisions require meaningful human control before deploying a system.

**Outcome**  
People remain accountable for the use of lethal force.

Chapter: The Change We Are Creating - Always in the Loop? The Arming and Autonomy of Robots

## Practice 7: Hold robot creators responsible

**Problem**  
Design choices shape how robots behave after they leave the laboratory.

**Action**  
Require creators to examine the military uses and harms of their work.

**Outcome**  
Robots are designed with greater care for their human effects.

Chapter: The Change We Are Creating - Robotic Gods: Our Machine Creators

## Practice 8: Test ideas borrowed from science fiction

**Problem**  
Fiction can inspire useful inventions while hiding practical limits and dangers.

**Action**  
Separate imaginative possibilities from claims that evidence can support.

**Outcome**  
Creative ideas improve research without distorting judgment.

Chapter: The Change We Are Creating - What Inspires Them: Science Fiction's Impact on Science Reality

## Practice 9: Respect ethical refusal

**Problem**  
Technical workers may be pressured to support uses that violate their values.

**Action**  
Give roboticists a real way to reject projects they consider harmful.

**Outcome**  
Ethical concerns can shape technology before harm occurs.

Chapter: The Change We Are Creating - The Refuseniks: The Roboticists Who Just Say No

## Practice 10: Change organizations with the technology

**Problem**  
New tools create little value when old structures and habits control their use.

**Action**  
Redesign doctrine, training, and organization around the abilities of robotic systems.

**Outcome**  
Technology produces real operational change instead of small improvements.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - The Big Cebrowski and the Real RMA: Thinking About Revolutionary Technologies

## Practice 11: Build tactics around human and robot strengths

**Problem**  
Robots perform poorly when forces use them as simple replacements for people.

**Action**  
Assign machines repetitive or dangerous tasks while people handle judgment and uncertainty.

**Outcome**  
Combined teams fight more effectively with less human exposure.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - Advanced Warfare: How We Might Fight with Robots

## Practice 12: Protect leadership in unmanned warfare

**Problem**  
A military can lose its advantage when internal interests resist disruptive systems.

**Action**  
Reward leaders who test unmanned methods and challenge programs that protect outdated roles.

**Outcome**  
Useful innovation survives resistance from established institutions.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - Robots That Don't Like Apple Pi: How the U.S. Could Lose the Unmanned Revolution

## Practice 13: Expect robots to spread beyond major powers

**Problem**  
Cheap parts and shared knowledge let small groups build military robots.

**Action**  
Plan defenses against improvised systems made from widely available technology.

**Outcome**  
Forces are less surprised when new users deploy robotic weapons.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - Open-Source Warfare: College Kids, Terrorists, and Other New Users of Robots at War

## Practice 14: Defend the electronic battlefield

**Problem**  
Robotic forces depend on signals and software that enemies can disrupt.

**Action**  
Protect communications, navigation, code, and power against attack or failure.

**Outcome**  
Robotic systems remain useful when electronic conflict begins.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - Losers and Luddites: The Changing Battlefields Robots Will Fight On and the New Electronic Sparks of War

## Practice 15: Account for human reactions to robots

**Problem**  
People may fear, trust, hate, or bond with machines in irrational ways.

**Action**  
Test how operators, civilians, and enemies respond emotionally to each system.

**Outcome**  
Deployment plans better predict human behavior around robots.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - The Psychology of Warbots

## Practice 16: Plan for war in public view

**Problem**  
Recorded robot strikes can shape public opinion apart from their military value.

**Action**  
Prepare to explain unmanned operations with timely facts and visible accountability.

**Outcome**  
Public debate rests on clearer evidence about the conflict.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - YouTube War: The Public and Its Unmanned Wars

## Practice 17: Protect operators from remote war stress

**Problem**  
Physical distance does not remove the emotional strain of repeated killing and surveillance.

**Action**  
Train and support remote operators for the unique stress of moving between combat and home life.

**Outcome**  
Operators can perform their duties with better mental health.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - Changing the Experience of War and the Warrior

## Practice 18: Preserve judgment under information pressure

**Problem**  
Constant data can overwhelm leaders and pull senior officers into minor decisions.

**Action**  
Set clear decision levels and filter information before it reaches commanders.

**Outcome**  
Leaders focus on choices that match their responsibility.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - Command and Control… Alt-Delete: New Technologies and Their Effect on Leadership

## Practice 19: Control access to remote force

**Problem**  
Remote technology allows more people and organizations to take part in combat.

**Action**  
Define who may operate military systems and under what authority.

**Outcome**  
Participation in war remains tied to clear responsibility.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - Who Let You in the War? Technology and the New Demographics of Conflict

## Practice 20: Apply the laws of war to every automated decision

**Problem**  
Software may fail to distinguish lawful targets or protect civilians in complex settings.

**Action**  
Test each system against legal duties before and during its use.

**Outcome**  
Automation supports compliance without replacing human legal responsibility.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - Digitizing the Laws of War and Other Issues of (Un)Human Rights

## Practice 21: Design for safe and ethical behavior

**Problem**  
A capable robot may act in harmful ways when its goals or controls fail.

**Action**  
Build limits, human overrides, and failure tests into every autonomous system.

**Outcome**  
Robots remain easier to control when errors or conflicts arise.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - A Robot Revolt? Talking About Robot Ethics

## Practice 22: Judge robots by how humans use them

**Problem**  
The same robotic capability can protect people or increase violence.

**Action**  
Evaluate each use through its purpose, rules, and effects on human life.

**Outcome**  
Society gains the benefits of robots while limiting their harms.

Chapter: What Change Is Creating For Us - Conclusion: The Duality of Robots and Humans
