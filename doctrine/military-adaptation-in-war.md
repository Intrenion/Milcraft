## Practice 1: Build the ability to adapt before war

**Problem**  
Military organizations often resist change when their habits and beliefs feel secure.

**Action**  
Create habits of honest study, open debate, and rapid learning before combat begins.

**Outcome**  
The force can respond faster when war exposes false assumptions.

Chapter: Introduction: The Background to Military Adaptation

## Practice 2: Study adaptation in its historical setting

**Problem**  
Lessons from past wars become misleading when people ignore the conditions under which they were drawn.

**Action**  
Examine how each force changed within its political, social, technical, and military setting.

**Outcome**  
Historical lessons become more useful for judging new problems.

Chapter: The Historical Framework of Adaptation

## Practice 3: Combine many small changes into a working system

**Problem**  
New weapons or tactics fail when other parts of the force do not change with them.

**Action**  
Test and connect changes in tactics, technology, training, command, and supply.

**Outcome**  
The force develops methods that work together under battlefield conditions.

Chapter: Complex Adaptation: The Western Front: 1914 - 1918

## Practice 4: Test operational success against strategic needs

**Problem**  
Early battlefield victories can hide weaknesses in intelligence, logistics, production, and strategy.

**Action**  
Judge every operational method by whether it supports the full demands of the war.

**Outcome**  
The force avoids building its plans around strengths that cannot deliver lasting success.

Chapter: Flawed Adaptation: German Adaptation: The Opening Battles of World War II

## Practice 5: Build an integrated defense and improve it with evidence

**Problem**  
Separate defensive units cannot use limited resources well against a changing attack.

**Action**  
Connect detection, command, communication, and combat units, and use battle results to improve the system.

**Outcome**  
Defenders can concentrate the right forces at the right time.

Chapter: The Battle for the British Isles: June 1940 - May 1941

## Practice 6: Measure real effects and answer enemy changes

**Problem**  
Air forces waste lives and resources when doctrine rests on untested claims about bombing and defense.

**Action**  
Collect reliable combat evidence and adjust tactics as the enemy changes its defenses.

**Outcome**  
Air operations become more effective against an opponent who is also learning.

Chapter: Adaptation in the Air War: RAF Bomber Command and the Luftwaffe's Air Defenses (15 May 1940 - 7 May 1945)

## Practice 7: Recover from surprise through fast battlefield learning

**Problem**  
A force can suffer severe losses when old beliefs prevent it from recognizing new threats.

**Action**  
Let commanders revise tactics quickly and restore cooperation among combat arms.

**Outcome**  
The force can regain effectiveness before the enemy turns surprise into victory.

Chapter: The 1973 Yom Kippur War

## Practice 8: Make adaptation a permanent command duty

**Problem**  
Future wars will punish forces that wait for certainty before changing.

**Action**  
Reward honest reporting, informed dissent, realistic testing, and timely decisions at every command level.

**Outcome**  
The organization can change faster without losing direction or control.

Chapter: Conclusion: Adaptation and the Future
