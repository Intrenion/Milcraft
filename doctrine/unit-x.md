## Practice 1: Connect military users with technology builders

**Problem**  
Military needs often become unclear when users and builders work apart.

**Action**  
Place military users and technology builders on one team to solve a specific operational need.

**Outcome**  
The team produces technology that works in real operations.

Chapter: Unit X

## Practice 2: Learn from failure without hiding it

**Problem**  
Teams repeat costly mistakes when they hide failed decisions.

**Action**  
Record each failure and use its cause to guide the next decision.

**Outcome**  
Future decisions become safer and more effective.

Chapter: Zeroized

## Practice 3: Test simple prototypes with real users

**Problem**  
Long planning cycles can produce systems that do not meet field needs.

**Action**  
Give a simple prototype to real users and improve it from their feedback.

**Outcome**  
Useful systems reach the field faster.

Chapter: The Gonkulator

## Practice 4: Shorten the path from detection to action

**Problem**  
Slow information flow gives an adversary more time to move or strike.

**Action**  
Connect sensors, analysis, decisions, and weapons in one fast process.

**Outcome**  
Forces can respond before a target escapes or attacks.

Chapter: A New Kill Chain for North Korea

## Practice 5: Make innovation part of the institution

**Problem**  
A temporary innovation team cannot create lasting change from the edge of an organization.

**Action**  
Give the team a permanent mission, stable authority, and access to senior leaders.

**Outcome**  
New technology becomes part of normal defense operations.

Chapter: Unit X Loses the X

## Practice 6: Set clear rules for military automation

**Problem**  
Automated systems can cause harm when responsibility for their decisions is unclear.

**Action**  
Assign human responsibility for every important use of military automation.

**Outcome**  
Forces can use machines while keeping people accountable.

Chapter: Washington and the Rise of the Machines

## Practice 7: Give private investors a reason to fund defense technology

**Problem**  
Startups cannot survive on small trials without a path to large contracts.

**Action**  
Connect successful trials to clear purchase plans that attract private capital.

**Outcome**  
More startups can build and supply useful defense technology.

Chapter: Venture Capital Goes to War

## Practice 8: Adapt commercial technology during combat

**Problem**  
Battlefield needs change faster than traditional weapons programs can respond.

**Action**  
Update commercial drones, software, and communications from direct battlefield feedback.

**Outcome**  
Forces adapt faster than the enemy.

Chapter: Ukraine and the Battlefield of the Future

## Practice 9: Build military advantage through software

**Problem**  
Hardware alone cannot keep pace with threats that change quickly.

**Action**  
Design military systems so software can improve their abilities after deployment.

**Outcome**  
Existing equipment stays useful as missions and threats change.

Chapter: From Steel to Silicon
